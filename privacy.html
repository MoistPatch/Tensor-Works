Privacy Policy

Last updated: 16 May 2026

1. Introduction

Tensor Works ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your personal information and complying with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose and protect your personal information when you use our website at tensorworks.online or engage with us in connection with our products and services.

By using our website or submitting an enquiry, you agree to the collection and use of your information as described in this policy.

2. Information We Collect

We collect personal information that is reasonably necessary to provide our services. This may include:

  • Contact information: your full name, work email address, phone number, and postal address.
  • Organisation details: company or department name, sector, and ABN where provided.
  • Enquiry and project details: information you provide in enquiry forms, including hardware requirements, budget context, project timelines, and technical specifications.
  • Account information: if you create a Shopify customer account, we collect login credentials (managed by Shopify), order history, and saved addresses.
  • Transaction data: records of purchases, quotes, and order fulfilment details.
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, referring URLs, and pages visited, collected automatically via server logs and analytics.

We do not collect sensitive information (such as health information, financial account numbers, or government identifiers) unless you voluntarily provide it and it is necessary for the services requested.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information for the following purposes:

  • To respond to hardware enquiries and provide product availability and pricing information.
  • To prepare and deliver formal quotations for enterprise hardware and software products.
  • To process and fulfil orders placed through our website.
  • To manage your customer account and provide account-related services.
  • To communicate with you about your order, shipment status, or enquiry.
  • To send transactional notifications (order confirmations, delivery updates).
  • To improve our website, product catalogue, and customer experience.
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations.

We will not use your personal information for unsolicited direct marketing without your consent. Where you have provided consent, you may withdraw it at any time by contacting us.

4. Disclosure of Your Information

We may share your personal information with the following categories of third parties, strictly for the purposes described above:

Shopify Inc.

Our online store is powered by Shopify. Shopify acts as a data processor on our behalf and handles payment processing, customer account management, and order fulfilment infrastructure. Shopify's privacy policy is available at shopify.com/legal/privacy. Shopify may store and process data in the United States and other jurisdictions.

Dicker Data Pty Ltd

We fulfil hardware orders through Dicker Data, Australia's largest technology distributor. Where necessary for order fulfilment, we may share your name, delivery address, and order details with Dicker Data.

Cloudflare, Inc.

Our website and API functions are served via Cloudflare's network. Cloudflare may process request metadata (including IP addresses) in the ordinary course of providing CDN and security services. Cloudflare's privacy policy is available at cloudflare.com/privacypolicy.

Web3Forms

Enquiry forms on our website are processed using Web3Forms (web3forms.com), which receives form submission data including your name, email, organisation, and enquiry details in order to forward the enquiry to us by email.

Netlify, Inc.

Our website is hosted on Netlify's platform. Netlify may process technical data (including request logs and IP addresses) in the course of providing hosting services.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for their own marketing purposes.

5. International Transfers

Some of our service providers (including Shopify, Cloudflare, Web3Forms, and Netlify) operate outside Australia and may store or process your information in overseas jurisdictions including the United States and European Union. We take reasonable steps to ensure these providers have adequate data protection practices consistent with Australian privacy law.

6. Data Security

We implement reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information from unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These measures include HTTPS encryption, access controls, and use of reputable, security-certified infrastructure providers.

While we take data security seriously, no system is completely secure. If you become aware of a security incident affecting your information, please contact us immediately.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to provide our services, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Enquiry data is typically retained for up to 7 years consistent with Australian tax and record-keeping requirements. You may request earlier deletion of your information by contacting us (subject to legal retention requirements).

8. Cookies and Tracking

Our website uses browser localStorage and sessionStorage to maintain cart state and cache API responses. We do not use third-party advertising cookies. Standard server access logs may be maintained by our hosting provider. Where analytics are in use, they are anonymised and aggregated.

9. Your Rights

Under the Australian Privacy Act 1988, you have the right to:

  • Request access to the personal information we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate, out-of-date, or incomplete information.
  • Make a complaint about how we handle your personal information.
  • Withdraw consent to direct marketing communications.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below. We will respond within 30 days.

10. Complaints

If you believe we have not handled your personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 or this policy, you may lodge a complaint with us in writing. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may escalate the matter to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au.
